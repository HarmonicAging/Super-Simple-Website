FAQ

Have questions or feedback about our vibration treatments? Here we address the pros, cons, and common questions.

1. What is vibration treatment?

Vibration treatment is a process that uses controlled vibrations to simulate the natural playing process of a stringed instrument, enhancing its resonance and tone.

2. How does the process work?

We use a tactile transducer to apply vibrations to your instrument. These vibrations mimic the effects of playing the instrument, accelerating the break-in process and improving sound quality.

3. Is vibration treatment safe for my instrument?

Yes, our process is safe for all types of stringed instruments, including guitars, violins, cellos, and upright basses. We use controlled settings to ensure the integrity of your instrument.

4. How long does the treatment take?

Typically, a full course of treatment involves 20-30 hours of vibration, spread over multiple sessions. The exact duration can vary based on the instrument and desired results.

5. Can I do the treatment myself at home?

Absolutely! We offer a Vibration Treatment Kit that includes everything you need to perform the treatment at home.

6. What kind of improvements can I expect?

Musicians report enhanced resonance, deeper tonal quality, and an overall more mature sound from their instruments after treatment.

7. Do you offer custom solutions?

Yes, we provide tailored vibration treatment solutions for luthiers and repair shops. Contact us for more details.

8. How much does the treatment cost?

Our in-shop treatments start at $50 per session. We also offer on-site treatments and custom solutions. Please refer to our Services page for detailed pricing.

9. What should I do to prepare my instrument for treatment?

Loosen the strings slightly and ensure the instrument is clean. We'll take care of the rest!

10. How can I schedule a treatment?

You can schedule an appointment through our website or contact us directly to arrange a convenient time.
